AI Foundations.

A program built around the belief that how we use AI matters as much as whether we use it.

AI is already here.

The question is no longer whether to use it. It is whether we use it well, with intention, and in a way that amplifies what makes us distinctive and increases the judgment, creativity, and impact of the people in this building.

This is DCP's plan for doing that. A program built around the belief that people are what makes any of this work in the first place.

Three things are happening in parallel: a conversation series designed to build real skill and provoke thinking, a certification that gives everyone a shared foundation, and a clear map of what tools are available and how to access them.

This is not a one-size-fits-all program. It is something we are building together, and there is space to learn, experiment, and engage at your own pace.

Our approach

Three components of the program.

01
The Conversations
A series of sessions across DCP. Panels, debates, live showcases and structured conversations. Some sessions build specific skills, others deepen how you can think and what you can do with AI.
02
The Certification
A self-directed course that builds foundations that everyone at DCP needs to use AI responsibly and well. A significant focus is on generative AI guardrails and safety: how to protect ourselves collectively, protect client confidentiality, and avoid the kinds of misuse that create real risk.
03
Tool Access
Links and descriptions of what AI tools are available to you right now. Links, access levels, and the ability to request access.

The foundation

DCP Certification.

The DCP AI Foundations Certification provides a baseline for every person at DCP to use AI responsibly and well. You'll learn what AI physically is, how it gets built and how to shape it.

A significant portion covers generative AI guardrails and safety including client confidentiality, intellectual property questions, and when to ask for help before something becomes a problem.

It is self-paced and self-directed. There is a quiz at the end and a completion certificate.

What you have access to

Tool Access.

These are the AI tools available to DCP staff right now. Both are enterprise-licensed, which means your data is protected and the agency has negotiated the terms under which they operate. Use these. Do not use personal or consumer alternatives for client work.

Microsoft Copilot
Your AI assistant inside Microsoft 365. Copilot works across Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams. It can draft documents, summarize email threads, analyze data, and build personal agents that automate recurring tasks. The DCP enterprise license also gives you access to Claude (Anthropic) and ChatGPT (OpenAI) models through the Microsoft environment, with enterprise data protections in place. Your data stays inside the DCP tenant.
Access Copilot
Available to all DCP staff with a Microsoft 365 license. If you do not see Copilot in your apps, contact IT.
Adobe Firefly
Adobe's generative AI model built for creative work. Firefly generates images, vectors, and design elements from text prompts and is trained on licensed content, which matters for commercial use. Firefly Boards is a shared creative workspace where teams run Firefly together in real time. Beyond Adobe's own models, Firefly also surfaces partner models inside the same environment: Google Imagen and Veo for image and video generation, and Runway for video. Partner models are third-party AI systems that Adobe has integrated directly into Firefly so you can access multiple generation tools without leaving the platform. Available inside Creative Cloud and through the Firefly web app.
Access Firefly
Available through your Adobe Creative Cloud license. Firefly Boards is built for team use. Talk to your creative director or producer to get started.
